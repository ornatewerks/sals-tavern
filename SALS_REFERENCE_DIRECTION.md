# Sal's Tavern — Reference-Based Redesign Direction

## Verdict on v1
The first concept looked like AI slop because it tried to synthesize “dark tavern” from generic ingredients: faux texture, vague premium language, generic sectioning, and invented atmosphere. The next direction should be more restrained, more typographic, more specific to Sal's, and more inspired by actual best-in-class hospitality sites.

## Reference set inspected

### 1. Bemelmans Bar / The Carlyle
URL: https://www.rosewoodhotels.com/en/the-carlyle-new-york/dining/bemelmans-bar

Why it matters:
- Feels like an institution, not a marketing landing page.
- Uses a split hero: left side identity/copy/actions, right side atmospheric interior imagery.
- Serif typography and large negative space make the venue feel expensive.
- Practical actions are clear: Reserve, Inquire, View Menu, 360 Tour.
- The page sells memory and ritual: murals, piano, late-night jazz, social history.

What Sal's should borrow:
- Treat Sal's like a beloved local institution.
- Use a left rail / information column with hours, call, directions, menu.
- Use large editorial serif headings.
- Build around real tavern rituals: regulars, game nights, cold beer, fish fry/specials, local stories.
- The design can be quiet and confident; it does not need fake neon everywhere.

What not to borrow:
- Do not make Sal's feel like an Upper East Side hotel bar.
- Do not use luxury-hotel language or overly precious copy.

### 2. Da Maria — Awwwards restaurant concept
URL: https://www.awwwards.com/sites/da-maria

Why it matters:
- Strong editorial “printed menu/poster” system.
- Giant type does most of the work.
- Warm off-white paper, red accent, ornamental texture, stamped details.
- Feels designed without feeling overcomplicated.

What Sal's should borrow:
- A tavern handbill / printed-menu language.
- Cream paper, ink, red/green accent, stamped badges, rule lines.
- Make specials feel like a designed artifact, not a generic card grid.

What not to borrow:
- Do not copy Roman restaurant glamour.
- Do not use generic food photography if real assets are not available.

### 3. Death & Co
URL: https://www.deathandcompany.com/

Why it matters:
- Strong dark mood and dramatic bar environment.
- Logo/mark centered over photography.
- Location and private-event actions are simple.

What Sal's should borrow:
- Dark, atmospheric hero treatment.
- Let the logo/identity sit confidently rather than surrounding it with clutter.
- Use warm practical lighting: bar lamps, amber glow, beer signs, wood.

What not to borrow:
- Do not overdo black texture or theatrical cocktail-bar mystique.
- Sal's is a neighborhood tavern, not a speakeasy brand.

### 4. Dante NYC
URL: https://www.dante-nyc.com/

Why it matters:
- Proven bar/restaurant IA: Welcome, Story, Menus, Reservations, Gifts, Locations.
- Hero can be a single strong product/venue image with simple brand hierarchy.
- Awards/history are used as credibility, not filler.

What Sal's should borrow:
- Lead with heritage and place.
- Simple top nav: Menu, Specials, Events, Visit, Call.
- If Sal's has history, established date, press, local awards, or legacy details, feature them.

What not to borrow:
- Do not leave huge empty black space or rely on photography alone.

### 5. Eleven Madison Park
URL: https://www.elevenmadisonpark.com/

Why it matters:
- Extreme restraint: logo, nav, one image, direct booking.
- White space makes the brand feel confident.

What Sal's should borrow:
- Fewer sections above the fold.
- Less copy, stronger hierarchy.
- One clear primary action.

What not to borrow:
- Too sterile/minimal for a small-town tavern.
- Sal's needs warmth, texture, and approachability.

### 6. The Connaught / Maybourne hospitality system
URL: https://www.maybourne.com/en/hotels/the-connaught

Why it matters:
- Expensive editorial rhythm: large serif headings, cream field, plum/green accent, disciplined grids.
- Every section has a purpose: story, spaces, offers, restaurants, celebrations.

What Sal's should borrow:
- Premium spacing and section rhythm.
- Calm typography instead of loud fake effects.
- Structured cards for food, drinks, events, private parties.

What not to borrow:
- Do not make the page feel like a luxury hotel brochure.

## New direction: “The town tavern, treated like an institution”

Sal's should not look like a generic sports bar, fake dive bar, or upscale cocktail lounge. It should feel like the place everyone in town knows, but designed with the confidence of a national hospitality group.

### Visual language
- Background: warm paper cream and deep tavern green/oxblood, not full-time black.
- Typography: editorial serif for headings; sturdy condensed sans for utility labels.
- Layout: large split hero, poster-style specials, rule lines, stamps, small badges, generous white space.
- Texture: subtle paper grain and ink rules, not fake grunge.
- Imagery: if no real photos, use designed “tavern artifacts” — menu boards, matchbook marks, coasters, signs, receipt-style specials — until real photography exists.
- Motion: small premium touches only: hover reveals, marquee hours, subtle menu-tab interaction. No random animations.

### Homepage structure
1. Hero: split layout
   - Left: Sal's logo/name, one confident line, today’s hours, Call, Directions, View Menu.
   - Right: atmospheric tavern panel / designed interior collage / eventual real photo.
2. Institution strip
   - “Cold beer. Hot food. Regulars welcome. Troy/Albany-area tavern energy.”
   - Include local anchoring and established/history if available.
3. Handbill specials
   - Designed like a printed specials sheet, not cards.
4. Food & drink
   - Small, clear menu preview: wings/burgers/sandwiches/beer/cocktails.
5. Events / nights
   - Trivia, game night, live music, karaoke, darts/pool, if applicable.
6. Visit block
   - Address, hours, phone, map/directions, parking note.
7. Footer
   - No fluff. Call, menu, directions, social.

### Copy tone
Use plain, proud tavern language:
- “A good local bar should feel easy to walk into.”
- “Cold beer, hot food, familiar faces.”
- “Pull up a stool. Catch the game. Stay for one more.”
- “Tonight’s board” instead of “Curated offerings.”

Avoid:
- “Elevated culinary journey”
- “Handcrafted experience”
- “Where tradition meets modernity”
- Generic AI hospitality words

## Design north star
If Bemelmans is an iconic New York bar and Da Maria is an editorial restaurant poster, Sal's should become an iconic small-town tavern handbill brought to life on the web.

The target is not “luxury.” The target is “beloved local institution with expensive art direction.”
